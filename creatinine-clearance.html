Cockcroft-Gault Equation

The most widely used formula for estimating creatinine clearance (CrCl) as a surrogate for GFR in drug dosing decisions.

CrCl (mL/min) =
[(140 − age) × weight (kg)]
─────────────────────────
    72 × SCr (mg/dL)

× 0.85 for female patients

Weight Selection

The original equation was derived using Actual Body Weight (ABW). Weight selection matters clinically:

  • ABW — use when patient is at or below IBW
  • IBW — use when ABW is within 30% of IBW
  • AdjBW — use for obese patients (ABW > 130% of IBW)

IBW Formulas (Devine)

Male: 50 + 2.3 × (height in cm − 152.4) / 2.54
Female: 45.5 + 2.3 × (height in cm − 152.4) / 2.54

AdjBW Formula

AdjBW = IBW + 0.4 × (ABW − IBW)

CKD Staging (GFR-based)

  • G1: ≥ 90 — Normal or high
  • G2: 60–89 — Mildly decreased
  • G3a: 45–59 — Mildly-moderately decreased
  • G3b: 30–44 — Moderately-severely decreased
  • G4: 15–29 — Severely decreased
  • G5: < 15 — Kidney failure

Limitations

CrCl via Cockcroft-Gault may be less accurate in:

  • Extreme body weight (very obese or cachectic)
  • Rapidly changing renal function (AKI)
  • Children (< 18 years) — use Schwartz formula instead
  • Pregnancy
  • Amputees or patients with muscle-wasting conditions
⚠ CrCl is an estimate. Clinical context, labs, and professional judgment always take precedence.
